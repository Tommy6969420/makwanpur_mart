Our Story & Mission

Empowering Hetauda's Neighborhood Bazaar

Makwanpur Mart was born out of a simple observation in Hetauda: while megacity online retail giants like Daraz serve the nation, their 3-to-5 day shipping delays and impersonal return processes leave local shoppers frustrated. At the same time, hundreds of thriving family-owned stalls across Hetauda Wards 1 to 19 lacked a digital platform to reach neighborhood customers.

The Solo-Dev Bootstrapped Ethos

Unlike heavily funded tech startups that burn venture capital on predatory pricing, Makwanpur Mart is built and operated by a solo developer directly from Hetauda. We operate with zero outside capital and 3 months of self-sustained runway. Why? Because we focus on real, sustainable unit economics and personal community trust rather than vanity metrics.

Door-to-Door Vendor Onboarding

We don't ask traditional store owners to navigate complicated online portals alone. Our founder personally visits local shopkeepers door-to-door in Hetauda, verifying ward registration, explaining Electronic Commerce Act 2081 compliance, and providing hands-on training. This ensures every vendor listed on our platform is a genuine local business you can visit in person.

Platform Positioning: Neutral Mediator

Under Nepal's E-Commerce regulations, Makwanpur Mart acts as a dedicated technology intermediary. Vendors remain contractually obligated to ensure product quality, issue legal VAT/PAN invoices, and honor their published return policies. Our role is ensuring complete transparency, rapid delivery coordination, and an accessible, impartial grievance dispute process.
